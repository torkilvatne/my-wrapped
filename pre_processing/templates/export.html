Getting Started

How to load your listening history and export playlists to Spotify.

Part 1 — Your listening data
Step by step
1
Request your Spotify Extended Streaming History
Go to spotify.com/account/privacy and scroll down to Download your data. Tick Extended streaming history and click Request. Spotify will email you a download link — this usually takes a few days, but can take up to 30 days.
Make sure to request Extended streaming history, not just "Account data". The extended version includes every play from your full history; the basic version only covers the past year.
2
Download and unzip the archive
Once Spotify emails you, download the zip file and unzip it. Inside you'll find a folder called Spotify Extended Streaming History containing files named Streaming_History_Audio_*.json. That folder is what you point the script at.
3
Run the generator script
You need Python 3 — no extra packages. Download generate.py from the upload screen (click Don't have a data file yet?) and run:
python3 generate.py "path/to/Spotify Extended Streaming History"
To write the output to a specific folder:
python3 generate.py "path/to/data" ~/Desktop/my-wrapped
The script produces spotify_data.json in the output folder. That's the file you upload here.
4
Upload your data
If you're on the upload screen, click Choose file… and select spotify_data.json. Your data is stored in your browser's local storage (IndexedDB) — nothing is sent to any server. To load new data later, click ⚙ → Clear data and start over.
Part 2 — Export playlists to Spotify
Step by step
1
Create a Spotify Developer app
Go to developer.spotify.com/dashboard and log in with your Spotify account. Click Create app and fill in any name and description.
2
Set the redirect URI
In the Redirect URIs field, add the callback URL for the site you're using. The redirect URI is https://my-wrapped.torkil95.workers.dev/callback.html. If testing locally it's http://127.0.0.1:8765/callback.html. Click Save.
The redirect URI must match exactly — including the port number and path. Use 127.0.0.1, not localhost; Spotify treats them as different addresses.
3
Copy your Client ID
After saving, open the app's Settings page and copy the Client ID (a 32-character hex string). You do not need the Client Secret.
4
Enter the Client ID here
Click the ⚙ icon in the navigation bar, paste your Client ID into the Spotify Client ID field and click Save. It's stored in your browser's local storage and persists between sessions.
5
Connect your Spotify account
Click Connect Spotify in the navigation bar. You'll be redirected to Spotify to authorise the app. Accept — the app requests permission to create and modify playlists. After authorising you're sent back here automatically.
If you see "redirect_uri: Not matching configuration", make sure the redirect URI in your Spotify app settings matches the address you're currently using (check the URL bar and the ⚙ settings hint for the exact value to register).
6
Export a playlist
Export buttons appear on the By Year and Song Counts pages. Click one to open a modal showing the songs that will be added. Edit the playlist name if you like, then click Export →. The script searches Spotify for each track and adds them to a new private playlist in your account.
Not all tracks will be found. Some songs may be unavailable in your region, removed from Spotify, or under a slightly different title. The export modal reports how many were matched.
